5.1.1 Administer Users Help - User Types and Roles

OpenClinica has two levels of user privileges – global privileges and per-study privileges. Global privileges are assigned based on User Type, and control access to administrative modules for creating new studies, managing user accounts, and providing management of CRFs and Subject records across all studies in the OpenClinica system. Per-study privileges are assigned within a Study or Study Site to user accounts based on User Role, and control the user’s access to functionality for a particular study. A user account may have multiple (sometimes different) Roles in different studies, and must be assigned a role in at least one Study or Study Site.

This approach is designed to enable a scaleable and flexible model for large and small sites. Responsibility may be centralized or spread across multiple administrators, PIs, and coordinators.

User Type

1. User

Normal user accounts that may be assigned roles in individual studies and study sites.

2. Business Administrator

User accounts with access to the Business Admin module.

3. Technical Administrator

Technical Administrators have all the privileges of Business Administrators as well as the capability to manage other Technical and Business Administrator accounts. Business Administrators may not create, modify, or remove Technical Administrator accounts.

User Role

1. Data Entry Person

The Data Entry Person role provides access to OpenClinica’s Submit Data module. This allows users to view study-specific study protocols, events, and CRFs while having the ability to create subjects, update subject information, and submit data collected on these subjects for studies for which they have authorized access. RAs can validate data previously submitted for a subject through double-data entry.

2. Principal Investigators

The Principal Investigator role provides access to OpenClinica’s Extract Data module. Investigators can view pre-approved queries, create their own queries and download datasets generated.

3. Principal Investigator/Study Coordinator

The Principal Investigator/Study Coordinator role provides access to OpenClinica’s Manage Study module.
